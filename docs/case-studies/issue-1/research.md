# Issue 1 Case Study Research

## Request

Create a beautiful HTML/CSS resume for a copywriter using the repository data. The resume must print/export to PDF correctly, preserve proportions and colors, include tasteful animation for screen viewing, and keep personal data editable separately.

## Repository Evidence

The repository contains three writing samples:

- `cases/case 1`: Telegram warm-up post for nutritionist Liza Bogatyreva. Goal: handle the family-resistance objection to healthy eating and warm readers toward individual consultation.
- `cases/case 2`: VK post for Best Wedding agency. Goal: warm couples toward a free consultation by explaining four reasons to hire a wedding organizer.
- `cases/case 3`: VK sales post for nutrition blogger Ulyana. Goal: sell a paid breakfast guide through a keyword in community messages.

## Online Research Summary

Copywriter resumes are stronger when they behave like compact portfolios: they should include a clear specialization, portfolio link, writing formats, business context, and case summaries that explain the task and decision logic. Resume guidance commonly recommends presenting copywriting work as evidence of problem solving, not just as text samples.

For print/PDF export, modern CSS guidance points to:

- `@media print` for print-specific layout.
- `@page` for paper size and margins.
- `break-inside: avoid` on cards or case sections.
- `print-color-adjust: exact` and `-webkit-print-color-adjust: exact` where preserving backgrounds/colors matters.
- Physical units or controlled page widths for predictable PDF output.

## Case Study Framing

The resume uses a STAR-like structure adapted for missing numeric outcomes:

- Situation: client, audience, channel, and barrier.
- Task: commercial goal of the text.
- Action: copywriting approach and persuasive mechanics.
- Result proxy: what the text is designed to achieve when no factual performance data exists.

This avoids inventing metrics while still showing strategic thinking.
